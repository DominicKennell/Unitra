Governance Structure

Unitra operates under a system of direct democracy, where all citizens have an equal voice in decision-making. This governance model is designed to ensure transparency, inclusivity, and fairness, allowing every citizen to participate in shaping our nation’s policies and initiatives.

The Council of Citizens

The Council of Citizens is a key governing body in Unitra, composed of elected representatives who organize community discussions, manage proposals, and oversee the implementation of decisions. The Council’s primary role is to facilitate citizen engagement and ensure that all voices are heard.

  • Role of the Council: Moderates and organizes citizen proposals, ensures transparency, and fosters open dialogue on all major issues.
  • Leader of the Council: A rotating leadership position within the Council, responsible for guiding discussions, ensuring fair representation, and acting as a liaison with other governance bodies.
  • Terms: Council members are elected by citizens for one-year terms to ensure fresh perspectives and maintain a responsive governance body.

Citizen Participation

Unitra’s direct democracy empowers every citizen to propose, discuss, and vote on initiatives. Here’s how citizens can actively participate in governance:

  • Propose New Initiatives: Any citizen can suggest policies or initiatives addressing national issues, cultural projects, or technological advancements. The Council of Citizens reviews all proposals before they are presented for a community vote.
  • Vote on Proposals: Unitra operates on a popular vote system, where the proposal with the most support from citizens is enacted. Voting is open to all registered citizens.
  • Participate in Community Forums: Citizens are encouraged to join forums, town halls, and online discussions to debate ideas and share perspectives on key issues.

Transparent Decision-Making

Transparency is at the heart of Unitra’s governance. Every policy, proposal, and decision is documented and accessible to citizens through the following channels:

  • Open Proposal Archive: An online archive where citizens can view all submitted proposals, current discussions, and the outcomes of past votes.
  • Voting Records: All voting results are made publicly available, providing transparency and accountability in decision-making.
  • Annual Report: A comprehensive report summarizing the past year’s initiatives, council activities, and areas of focus for the coming year.

How to Get Involved

Active citizen participation is crucial to Unitra’s success as a direct democracy. Here’s how you can get involved:

  • Submit a Proposal: Share your ideas for new policies or initiatives by submitting a proposal form, available in your citizen dashboard.
  • Join the Council: Elections for the Council of Citizens are held annually. Citizens who wish to contribute more directly to governance are encouraged to run for council positions.
  • Engage in Discussions: Attend town halls, join online forums, and participate in council meetings to stay informed and contribute to ongoing conversations.
